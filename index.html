Ariana Grande


Ariana Grande is an American singer, songwriter, and actress.
She's known for her pop music, acting roles, and philanthropy.


Biography


Ariana Grande-Butera was born on June 26, 1993, in Boca Raton, Florida. She began her career in 2008 in the Broadway musical 13, before playing the role of Cat Valentine in the Nickelodeon television series Victorious (2010–2013) and in the spinoff Sam & Cat (2013–2014). She has also appeared in other theatre and television roles and has lent her voice to animated television and films. Grande's music career began in 2011 with the soundtrack Music from Victorious. In 2013, she released her first studio album Yours Truly, which entered atop the US Billboard 200. The album's lead single, "The Way", opened in the top 10 of the Billboard Hot 100, with critics comparing her wide vocal range to that of Mariah Carey. Grande's second studio album, My Everything (2014), also entered at number one in the US, and contained the hit singles "Problem" and "Break Free".


Grammy-winning pop star Ariana Grande is best known for the songs “7 Rings,” “Thank U, Next,” “Rain On Me” with Lady Gaga, and “We Can’t Be Friends (Wait For Your Love).” Grande started her career on the stage, landing a role in the Broadway musical 13 as a teenager, before making her big break as Cat Valentine on the Nickelodeon show Victorious and its spinoff Sam & Cat. The Florida native then dove headfirst into a chart-topping music career after her debut album Yours Truly came out in 2013. In total, she has released an impressive seven albums in nine years, earning two Grammy Awards in the process. Returning to acting and musicals, Grande stars as Glinda in the 2024 movie adaption of Wicked. Her performance earned her a 2025 Golden Globe nomination.


Ariana Grande-Butera was born on June 26, 1993, in Boca Raton, Florida. She started performing in local theater at age 8 and gained recognition in 2008 as Charlotte in Broadway's 13. Her significant breakthrough came in 2010 with the Nickelodeon series Victorious, where she played Cat Valentine. After the show ended in 2013, she starred in the spinoff Sam & Cat. In 2011, Grande signed with Republic Records and released her first single, “Put Your Hearts Up,” which she later criticized. She shifted her music style and released her debut album Yours Truly in 2013, hitting No. 1 on the Billboard 200. Her second album, My Everything (2014), earned her a Grammy nomination, followed by a successful tour. Her third album, Dangerous Woman, released in May 2016, also received critical acclaim. In 2017, following a tragic bombing at her Manchester concert, Grande organized a benefit show to support the victims. Throughout her career, she has been known for her powerful voice, iconic high ponytails, and impactful music.
